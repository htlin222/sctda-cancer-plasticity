\begin{abstract}
\noindent
Drug-tolerant persister cells drive relapse after EGFR-targeted
therapy in lung cancer. Lineage-tracing studies have shown that these
persisters reversibly cycle among multiple transcriptional states, but
current single-cell trajectory methods cannot quantify such cyclic
state transitions.
Here we apply topological data analysis, specifically persistent
homology and the Mapper algorithm, to longitudinal single-cell
RNA-seq data from five EGFR-mutant lung-cancer systems spanning cell
lines, patient-derived xenografts, and patient tumours. We show that
a topological measure of cyclic cell-state structure increases
monotonically with drug exposure across all systems, survives
rigorous controls for cell-cycle confounding and batch effects, and
replicates in a 14-patient longitudinal cohort.
Loop-associated genes are enriched for epithelial--mesenchymal
transition programs, with significant overlap between cell-line and
xenograft models. Our results establish cyclic topological
plasticity as a reproducible, cross-scale phenotype of drug
tolerance and provide a reproducible workflow for detecting it in
other perturbation contexts.

\vspace{0.5em}
\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} topological data analysis; persistent homology;
Mapper; single-cell RNA-seq; drug tolerance; EGFR-mutant lung cancer;
cell-state plasticity; computational framework.
\end{abstract}
