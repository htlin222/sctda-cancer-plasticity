\section{Conclusions}

Drug treatment does not simply push lung-cancer cells along a one-way
road to resistance: it reshapes the cell-state landscape into a form
that admits inter-state cycling across EMT-like programs, consistent
with the cycling subpopulations established by lineage tracing in PC9
\citep{oren2021}. The maximum $H_1$ persistence
introduced here quantifies how robust that cycling becomes, rises
monotonically with osimertinib across cell-line and PDX validation
cohorts, survives two independent cell-cycle controls and Harmony
batch correction. Loop-associated genes are enriched for EMT
programs in both cell line and PDX when an EMT-aware Mapper filter
is used; an unbiased PC1 filter additionally surfaces cell-cycle
and proliferation genes, indicating the topological loops are
multi-program rather than purely EMT. We expect the readout to
generalise to other reversible-plasticity settings where standard
trajectory methods fall short.
