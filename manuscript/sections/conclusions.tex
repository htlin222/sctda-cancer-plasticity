\section{Conclusions}

Drug treatment does not simply push cancer cells along a one-way path
to resistance; it reshapes the cell-state landscape into a form that
admits reversible inter-state cycling. The topological framework
presented here quantifies this cyclic plasticity and reveals that it
increases monotonically with drug exposure across cell lines, xenografts,
and patient tumours. The approach generalises to any setting where cells
reversibly explore multiple transcriptional states, a phenomenon that
standard trajectory methods are designed to ignore.
